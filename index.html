The Title

“To forgive is wisdom, to forget is genius.
And easier. Because it's true.
It's a new world every heart beat.”
― Joyce Cary





"How happy is the blameless vessels lot!
The world forgetting by the world forgot."
- Alexander Pope



“Not the power to remember, but its very opposite,
the power to forget, is a necessary condition for our existence.”
― Sholem Asch



If we lose someone that meant so much to us, is it not fair that we wonder what it would be like to forget meeting them and having them in our lives? But what would that do for us? Is it worth forgetting the good just to subdue the bad? What are we really leaving behind besides the bad experiences?
